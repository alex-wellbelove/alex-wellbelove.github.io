Dependent types, simply

Dependent types have a reputation. When you read about them, you will hear that they are ‘a programming language feature so advanced that they can be used as a foundation of all of mathematics’ or ’that you need a PhD to use, let alone to understand, any language that has dependent types’. I’m continuing a tradition1 here by saying that they are actually simpler to understand than most type systems.

Cheapness

When talking about 'saving', I think a lot of people put it into the 'necessary evil' bucket. Something to be ashamed you're not quite doing enough of, or something that you're putting off a month or two. Something like a New Year's resolution to exercise more. But, in the same way that, depending on your outlook, exercise can be treadmill drudgery or a life-affirming hobby, I believe frugality can be in and of itself a source of happiness - without considering the financial benefits.
